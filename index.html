A practical reference. Click any section to expand. Grammar notes also appear in the Vocabulary Helper when you click a word in a story.

The most important rule: the verb always comes last. English says "I ate an apple." Kannada says "I apple ate." Every modifier and object stacks before the verb.

English (SVO) The dog ate biscuits.
→
Kannada (SOV) ನಾಯಿ biscuits ತಿಂದಿತು. naayi biscuits thindhithu.
Tip: When constructing a sentence, always finish with the verb. Any time clauses, relative clauses, and adjectives all precede the noun or verb they modify.

Kannada uses a 3-gender system for the third person (masculine / feminine / neuter). The 2nd person has a formal/informal split.

PersonSingularTranslit.PluralTranslit.
1stನಾನುnaanuನಾವುnaavu
2nd informalನೀನುneenuನೀವುneevu
2nd formalನೀವುneevuನೀವುneevu
3rd masc.ಅವನುavanuಅವರುavaru
3rd fem.ಅವಳುavaLuಅವರುavaru
3rd neuterಅದುadhuಅವುavu
Use ನೀವು (neevu) with elders, strangers, and in professional settings. ನೀನು (neenu) is for close friends and children. When in doubt, default to ನೀವು.

Verbs agree with the subject in gender, number, and person. The root stays fixed; the suffix changes. Below: ಮಾಡು (maaDu) — "to do".

SubjectVerbTranslit.Meaning
ನಾನು (I)ಮಾಡಿದೆmaaDidheI did
ನಾವು (We)ಮಾಡಿದೆವುmaaDidhevuWe did
ಅವನು (He)ಮಾಡಿದನುmaaDidanuHe did
ಅವಳು (She)ಮಾಡಿದಳುmaaDidaLuShe did
ಅದು (It / neuter)ಮಾಡಿತುmaaDituIt did
ಅವರು (They / you formal)ಮಾಡಿದರುmaaDidaruThey did
Pattern: Root + -idi- (past marker) + person/gender suffix. The neuter suffix -itu/-ithu is why verbs in the animal stories all end that way — animals take neuter agreement.

Instead of prepositions, Kannada adds suffixes directly to nouns to show their role. The noun root sometimes changes slightly before the suffix.

CaseSuffixRoleExampleMeaning
Nominative(none)Subjectನಾಯಿ ಓಡಿತುThe dog ran
Accusative-ಅನ್ನು (-annu)Direct objectನಾಯಿಯನ್ನು ನೋಡಿದೆI saw the dog
Dative-ಗೆ / -ಕ್ಕೆ (-ge / -kke)To / Forಅವಳಿಗೆ ಕೊಟ್ಟೆI gave (to) her
Genitive-ಅ / -ಇನ (-a / -ina)Of / possessionನನ್ನ ಮನೆMy house

Ablative-ಇಂದ (-inda)From / by means ofಊರಿಂದ ಬಂದೆI came from town
PatternExampleTranslit.Meaning
"X is not Y"ಅವನು ವಿದ್ಯಾರ್ಥಿ ಅಲ್ಲavanu vidyarthi allaHe is not a student
"There is no X"ಹಾಲು ಇಲ್ಲhaalu illaThere is no milk
"Did not"ನಾನು ತಿನ್ನಲಿಲ್ಲnaanu tinnalillaI did not eat

ಅಲ್ಲ (alla) negates identity or description — "X is not Y." ಇಲ್ಲ (illa) negates existence or a completed action — "X doesn't exist / didn't happen." These are not interchangeable.
